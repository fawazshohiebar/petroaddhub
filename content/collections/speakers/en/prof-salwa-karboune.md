Professor Salwa Karboune is the Dean of the Faculty of Agricultural and Environmental Sciences at McGill University and a full professor in the Department of Food Science and Agricultural chemistry. She also serves as the Scientific Director of The Consortium for Research, Innovation and Transformation of Agrifood (RITA).

Professor Karboune’s research focuses on the design and development of new "green" approaches for the synthesis of novel functional food ingredients that can improve both the quality and the functionality of food products. A key component of her multidisciplinary research work contributes to the sustainability of the agri-food sector, through the valorization of food-processing by-products into high-value ingredients. Her research program has garnered national and international recognition through its sustained excellence. Through numerous R&D collaboration with industries, she has led multiple large-scale partnership grants.

At the national and international level, she serves on several entities, including the CFI Science Advisory Board, CIFST’s Scientific Expert Council and INITIA Foundation. Professor Karboune is passionate about science and active learning, and she actively promotes hands-on projects. Under her mentorship, McGill student teams have earned first-place prizes at the prestigious IFT Food Product Development competition, reflecting her commitment to excellence in both research and education.
